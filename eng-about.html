Biography

At the Nexus of Science–Industry–Policy, Dr. Hong Xu has been bridging multidisciplinary expertise to advance the clean energy transition over the last decade, striving for energy efficiency and carbon neutrality. He earned a PhD in Renewable Energy at ETH Zurich and a Joint MSc in Materials Science from LMU and TU Munich supported by Erasmus Scholarship. He cultivated research skills at the Paul Scherrer Institute, European XFEL, and Polytechnic Institute of Paris, developing pioneering solutions in materials and energy sectors. With papers published, he achieved PSI-endorsed scientific highlights through 48h non-stop research campaigns, delivered ECS invited talks in Atlanta with travel grant, while fostering Next-Gen education through ETH master tutorials and TUM mentorship.

Refueling innovation towards sustainable industrial impact, he has collaborated with multinational companies in Europe and Asia, namely Toyota, Freudenberg, and Infineon, contributing to advanced energy materials solutions with authored patents. He has actively driven technology transfer in fuel cell imaging from Swiss PSI to Toyota Japan, and led technical training in water conservancy bridging ENPC Paris and HRWC Tianjin under the Franco-Chine cooperation. Alongside his engineering pursuits, he undertook the Executive Program at the Cambridge Judge Business School with a Santander scholarship, gaining practical experience at CITIC Securities and the former Y-Combinator in China.

A heart for catalyzing the global energy transition, he has engaged with Salzburg Global and St. Gallen Symposium, conducting policy research on hydrogen, critical minerals, and e-waste. He also served as an Expert Group Member at UNECE in Geneva and chaired session at UN Climate Change Conference hosted by UNFCCC in Bonn, advocating for the UN SDGsN° 7, 9, and 13. Through the lens of digital sustainability, he deeply seeks to integrate cross-domain knowledge graphs into tangible AI-driven solutions and shape global energy policy toward a resilient and equitable future.
